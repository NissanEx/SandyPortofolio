About

Society watches, quietly suffocating under the weight of favoritism dressed as destiny. It isn't merit that opens doors—but bloodlines, connections, whispers exchanged behind velvet curtains. How amusing, really… that people still pretend effort matters, when the game was rigged long before they were born.

There's a peculiar kind of despair in realizing that no matter how hard one struggles, someone else is handed victory simply for existing in the "right" circle. And yet, the world continues spinning, as if this quiet injustice is nothing more than a trivial inconvenience. Perhaps that's the cruelest part—not the nepotism itself, but how easily it is accepted, normalized… forgotten.

Joan of Arc

was a legendary French warrior and national hero who led armies in the 15th century. Born a peasant girl, she led the French army against the English during the Hundred Years' War at a young age (16–18). She was recognized for her bravery, particularly at the Battle of Orleans, before her tragic end.
